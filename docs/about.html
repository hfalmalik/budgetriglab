About BudgetRigLab

BudgetRigLab exists because most “budget gaming gear” articles are written by people who haven’t looked at a price tag since 2019. We cover mice, keyboards, headsets, monitors, and controllers that real people can actually afford — usually under $50, almost never over $150.

What we believe

Cheap gear has real tradeoffs, and pretending otherwise helps nobody. A $25 headset will not sound like a $150 one. Our job is to tell you which compromises are fine (plastic build, basic software) and which ones ruin the experience (mushy switches, 60 Hz panels for competitive shooters).

Diminishing returns kick in fast. The jump from a $10 office mouse to a $30 gaming mouse is enormous. The jump from $30 to $150 is mostly weight, wireless, and bragging rights. We help you find the point on that curve where spending more stops making sense for you.

Specs are a starting point, not the answer. Marketing numbers like “50 million click durability” and “7.1 surround sound” are often meaningless. We focus on the things that actually change how gear feels: sensor consistency, switch type, panel response, comfort over long sessions.

How our recommendations work

Articles on this site are drafted with AI assistance and edited around a consistent editorial standard: every recommendation must name a specific product, state its realistic street price, and list at least one honest downside. If an article can’t tell you why not to buy something, it doesn’t go live.

Product prices mentioned in articles are typical street prices at the time of writing. Budget gear prices swing constantly with sales — always check the current price before buying.
