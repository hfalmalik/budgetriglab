Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: July 4, 2026

BudgetRigLab is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com.

As an Amazon Associate, we earn from qualifying purchases.

What this means in plain English

  • Some links on this site point to products on Amazon. These are affiliate links.
  • If you click one of those links and buy something (the linked product or anything else during that session), Amazon pays us a small percentage of the sale.
  • You pay exactly the same price whether you use our link or not. The commission comes out of Amazon’s side, not yours.

What it does NOT mean

  • We are not paid by manufacturers to recommend their products.
  • Nobody sends us free gear in exchange for coverage.
  • Our commission is the same small percentage whether you buy the $20 pick or the $60 pick, so we have no incentive to upsell you — and you’ll notice we often recommend the cheaper option.

Accuracy

Prices quoted in articles are typical street prices at the time each article was written. Budget peripherals go on sale constantly, and prices you see on Amazon may be higher or lower. Always confirm the current price, seller, and product version on the retailer’s page before purchasing. Product specifications can also change between hardware revisions; when in doubt, verify on the manufacturer’s site.

Why we disclose this

The FTC requires that sites disclose material connections between themselves and the products they link to, and Amazon’s Associates Program Operating Agreement requires a clear statement on every page that contains affiliate links. That’s why you see the short disclosure line at the top of every page on this site.
