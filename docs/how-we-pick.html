How We Pick and Test

Every recommendation on BudgetRigLab has to survive the same process before it appears in an article. This page explains that process, so you can judge for yourself how much weight to give our picks.

Our selection process

  1. Spec verification. We start from manufacturer spec sheets and cross-check them against retailer listings and independent teardowns or benchmark databases. If a spec can’t be verified in at least two places, we don’t repeat it.
  2. Aggregated user-review analysis. We read across hundreds of verified-purchase reviews per product and weight long-term reports (6+ months of ownership) far above day-one impressions. Recurring failure patterns — double-clicking switches, dead LED zones, fraying cables — disqualify a product or get called out as the honest downside.
  3. Price tracking. “Budget” means the real street price, not MSRP. We track typical sale prices and tell you when a pick only makes sense on sale.
  4. The downside rule. Every product we recommend must ship with at least one honest downside in the article. If we can’t find a real tradeoff, we haven’t researched it enough to recommend it.

What we don’t do

  • We don’t accept free review units, sponsored placements, or manufacturer payments. Ever.
  • We don’t rank products by commission rate. Our affiliate links pay the same small percentage regardless of which product you choose.
  • We don’t claim hands-on lab testing we haven’t done. Where our analysis is based on verified specs and aggregated owner reports rather than in-house testing, that’s exactly what it is.

AI assistance disclosure

BudgetRigLab uses AI tools to help research, draft, and maintain articles. Every article follows the editorial rules above — verified specs, real street prices, mandatory downsides — and the site owner reviews the pipeline’s output and corrections.

Updates and corrections

“Best X” recommendations rot fast. We revisit articles when prices move, models are discontinued, or better options appear, and we mark meaningful revisions with an updated date. Spotted an error — a wrong spec, a dead link, a price that’s way off? Tell us and we’ll fix it: see the About page for contact details.
